Getting Started With Magikcraft

Welcome to Magikcraft (video)

School of Magik Slack

Sometimes you need help, or just to share ideas. The School of Magik is a place for that.

The School of Magik is a Slack team. Slack is a messaging app for teams and communities. In the School of Magik you can chat with the developers of Magikcraft and with other students.

To get an invitation to the School of Magik Slack team:

  1. Open slack.magikcraft.io in your browser.
  2. Enter your email address on the page.
  3. Check your inbox for the invitation.

To enter the School of Magik Slack, once you have your invitation:

  1. Open schoolofmagik.slack.com in your browser.

Log on to Magikcraft

  1. You need a Minecraft account - you can get that from Mojang.com.
  2. Download the Minecraft client.
  3. Create a new profile.
  4. In the new profile, select "Use version 1.10".
  5. Save the profile.
  6. Click Play.
  7. Click on "Multiplayer".
  8. Click on "Add server".
  9. For Server Name put Magikcraft Online
  10. For Server Address put world.magikcraft.io
  11. Click "Done".
  12. Double-click the Magikcraft Online server entry to connect.

You will appear in the tutorial level.

Going through the Tutorial Level

Wander around and get a feel for the controls of Minecraft.

The tutorial level has the story of Magikcraft embedded in it.

Eventually you will discover a challenge in the world. When you find yourself stuck, hunt around for a spell!

Writing Your First Spell

Use one of the magik words that you find in Magikcraft as a Minecraft command.

To issue a Minecraft command, press the '/' key on your keyboard, then type the magik word. Press Enter, and you get a link to the spellbook. This link includes your username and also the magik word.

To use the link to the spellbook, press 'T', then click on the link with your mouse. This opens the spellbook in your web browser.

If Minecraft is full-screen, switch out of full-screen to see your browser.

The spellbook includes examples when you open it using a magik word.

Here's an example of a spell.

  1. The first thing you need is magik, and you get that from magikcraft.io. Type the following into the spellbook:
var magik = magikcraft.io;
  1. Now you will create a function. A function is a magik spell that allows you to do things using special words. We are going to create a jump spell:
function jump(power){

}

The parens () and the curly braces {} are important parts of spells in JavaScript. If they are not done right, then the spell will fail! So take care.

  1. Now we will put the magik spell we found into our new spell:
function jump(power){
	magik.exsultus(power);
}

You've got parens () again, and a semi-colon ;
The semi-colon goes at the end of a line in a spell, like a full-stop goes at the end of a sentence.

  1. Now we invoke the spell:
function jump(power){
	magik.exsultus(power);
}
magik.invoco(jump);

This will invoke your jump spell. So all together you should now have this:

var magik = magikcraft.io;

function jump(power){
	magik.exsultus(power);
}
magik.invoco(jump);
  1. Now press the Play button in your spell book. After a few seconds, you should see this in the black box on the right-hand side:
You jump with some power!

If you see something else, then double-check your spell again.

  1. Press the Save button to save your spell. You should see:
Saved jump.js

Congratulations!

You just wrote your first spell. Now you can go back to Minecraft and cast it.

Cast Your Spell in Minecraft

  1. Go to Minecraft.
  2. Type: /cast jump 3

/cast is the command to cast spells in Magikcraft.
jump is the name of the spell that you are casting.
3 is the power to pass into the jump spell.

  1. Press Enter.

Exsultus!

You now jump in the air!

Not working?

  1. Go back and double-check the spell code.
  2. Also - double-check that you logged into the spellbook using your Minecraft username, spelt exactly the same as in Minecraft.
  3. Still not working? Go to the School of Magik Slack and ask for help there!

The Spellbook

The Spellbook is at http://spellbook.magikcraft.io.

You can write your spells here, and also test them.

Each page of the spellbook can hold only one spell. Don't try to put more than one spell on a page!

You can create a new spell by deleting the spell that is in there already. Don't worry, it won't delete your spell from Minecraft if you saved it!

What Next?

Now that you've created a spell and cast it in Minecraft, you can take a look at the Magikcraft API Documentation.

Remember to wipe the contents of your spellbook to create a new spell.

Here's the boilerplate that you need for a spell:

var magik = magikcraft.io;

function _____(){

}
magik.invoco(_____);

Replace _____ with the name you want to use for your new spell, and write the spell code in the body of the function.

Check out the sample spells in the Magikcraft Github.
